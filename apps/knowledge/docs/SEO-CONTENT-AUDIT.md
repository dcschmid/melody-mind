# SEO Content Audit

## Scope

Frontmatter review for:

- `src/content/knowledge-en`
- `src/content/quizzes`

Focus:

- snippet quality
- duplicate framing
- search-intent clarity
- overly similar decade quiz descriptions

## Findings

### 1. Decade quiz descriptions were too formulaic

All decade quizzes used nearly identical phrasing:

- `Test your knowledge of ... music history`
- then a short list of styles or artists

That was understandable, but weak for differentiation in search results and internal link
previews.

### 2. One decade title repeated the same dramatic frame

- `1950s`: `When Music Changed Everything`
- `1960s`: `When Music Changed Everything`

This is not catastrophic, but it creates unnecessary similarity between two major landing
pages.

## Changes Applied

- Rewrote all decade quiz descriptions with more specific topical framing.
- Updated the 1960s article title to avoid duplicating the 1950s framing.

## Ongoing Rules

- Keep quiz descriptions direct and challenge-oriented.
- Mention the decade plus 2-4 defining movements, not long name lists by default.
- Avoid repeating the exact same emotional framing across decade hub titles.
- Prefer concise summaries that still signal cultural context, not only genre names.

## Next Candidates

- Shorten a few longer evolution titles if CTR later underperforms.
- Audit article descriptions for length consistency against Search Console data.
