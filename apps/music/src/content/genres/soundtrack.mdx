The soundtrack shelf gathers the albums that think in scenes first: flight, space, battle,
weather, landscapes, and instrumental scale that suggests a camera moving before anyone
says a word.

These records are cinematic without needing to be background music. They use motif,
texture, pacing, and silence to imply a world around the tracklist, so the listener can
sense the shape of a story even without dialogue. The drama often comes from motion
itself: something approaching, something vanishing, something too large to fit inside a
normal song.
