The jazz shelf is small but distinct: brass color, night air, Afro-Cuban movement, and
songs that feel built around rooms, streets, and memory rather than straight genre
display.

These albums use rhythm as setting. The arrangements leave space for tension, restraint,
and the kind of story that seems to happen between a final drink, an empty street, and the
first pale light before sunrise. Jazz gives these records permission to breathe, but it
also makes every pause count.
