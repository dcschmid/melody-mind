Jazz enters the MelodyMind catalog through rhythm, ensemble color, and records that leave
space between events. Bass, drums, piano, guitar, brass, and woodwinds take different
roles from album to album. One release may lean on Afro-Cuban percussion and tightly
arranged horns; another may favor a small late-night ensemble, restrained vocals, or
electronic textures.

Many of these albums use a city after dark as their frame, but the settings are not
interchangeable. Streets, clubs, coastlines, and hotel rooms shape the pace of the music
and the choices made by its characters. Themes return in altered form, giving the
arrangements a sense of memory rather than simply repeating a mood.

The performances are AI-generated and assembled into concept albums with deliberate
sequencing. They draw from swing, cool jazz, Latin jazz, soul jazz, fusion, and nu jazz
without claiming to document any one tradition. The focus is narrower: how harmony,
rhythm, and instrumental conversation can carry romance, solitude, tension, or release
across a complete record.
