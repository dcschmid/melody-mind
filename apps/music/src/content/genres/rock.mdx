The rock shelf is where MelodyMind Music lets guitars do narrative work. The riffs are not
only there for weight; they sketch the weather around a scene, the impatience in a room,
the long drive before a confession, or the pride before a fall.

Hard rock muscle, folk-rock grain, rock-opera scale, city stories, and stage myths sit
beside albums that need a chorus strong enough to hold a plot together. The best entries
have scenery around the noise: a harbor, a rehearsal room, a coastline, a cracked throne,
or a crowd waiting for the first chord to turn private pressure into public release.
