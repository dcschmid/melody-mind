The Gothic collection gathers records that use darkness as part of the story, not as a
surface treatment. Deep bass, distant guitars, solemn piano, cold synthesizers, and
orchestral passages frame characters caught between grief, desire, obsession, and memory.
The scale can be theatrical, but the strongest details are often small: an empty room, a
voice behind a wall, or an object that refuses to stay buried.

No single style defines the section. Gothic rock and darkwave sit beside post-punk, dark
cabaret, symphonic metal, and atmospheric electronic music. Some albums unfold like
supernatural dramas; others keep their hauntings psychological and close to everyday life.
Recurring symbols and musical themes link the tracks, so a melody or image can change as
the story moves forward.

Every release is AI-assisted and developed as a complete album. The appeal lies in the
tension between beauty and unease: songs that remain melodic while allowing romance,
isolation, and fear to keep their sharper edges.
