The punk shelf is direct by design. These albums deal in borders, propaganda, bills, rent,
power, public anger, and the moment when a crowd stops asking for permission. The writing
works best when it stays close to pressure that can be named.

The sound is raw and compact: dry drums, dirty guitars, shouted hooks, sirens, and
fragments of broadcast noise. The politics are strongest when they refuse to float above
everyday detail, because a late notice, a checkpoint, or a locked door can say more than a
slogan shouted twice.
