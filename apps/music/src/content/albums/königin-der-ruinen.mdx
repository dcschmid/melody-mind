## About This Album

Königin der Ruinen is a German-language concept album by MelodyMind, rooted in Deutschrock
and Gothic Rock with dark pop sensibilities. Heavy guitars, pounding drums, cinematic
string layers and expressive vocals trace the story of a woman who returns to a city she
once loved — now broken, haunted, and filled with old wounds.

The album opens with "Zurück in diese Stadt," where the protagonist steps back into the
ruins of what was. "Asche an den Händen" confronts guilt and complicity, while "Dein Name
im Beton" wrestles with a name carved into concrete — a memorial to something lost.
"Schwarze Fenster" paints the city as a ghost: empty glass, dark rooms, memories that
refuse to fade.

"Ich brenne leise" and "Kein Thron aus Gold" turn inward anger into controlled strength —
not rage, but refusal. The title track "Königin der Ruinen" stands as the album's
emotional center: a gothic rock anthem about standing tall in a destroyed world without
needing glamour, revenge or approval. She is a queen not because she rules others, but
because she finally belongs to herself.

"Risse im Asphalt" and "Unter roten Wolken" push deeper into the urban wasteland — cracked
streets, blood-red skies, a landscape that mirrors the protagonist's internal state. "Was
von uns blieb" asks what survives after love collapses, and "Narben aus Licht" begins the
slow turn toward healing. "Mauern werden Staub" follows the walls breaking down, literally
and emotionally.

The album closes with "Neue Straßen" and "Aus Ruinen wächst ein Morgen" — not triumphant,
but quiet. The scars remain. They no longer define her as broken.

Musically, the album moves between intimate gothic ballads, powerful rock anthems and
atmospheric dark pop moments. The ruined city is more than a setting — it is a mirror of
the protagonist's emotional landscape.
