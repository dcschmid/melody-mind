## About This Album

The album opens with "Sous les Réverbères" and stays in the same register throughout: soft
piano, subtle accordion, brushed percussion. Track 4 carries the title track and the
record's clearest statement — Paris after midnight, the Seine lit up, nobody home. "Les
Fenêtres Éteintes" is the longest piece on the record and the most orchestral. "Ton Nom
dans la Bruine" closes things quietly, rain and all.

The lyrics are in French throughout. Some feel like unsent letters ("Une Lettre Jamais
Envoyée"), others like conversations replayed under streetlamps. There's heartbreak in
here, but the kind that ended quietly rather than dramatically. "Le Café Ferme Trop Tôt"
names the feeling quickest: the café closed before you were ready to leave.

This is not an album that reaches for grand romantic gestures. The piano shimmers, the
accordion sits in the background, and the vocals stay intimate. The production keeps
everything at the same late-night level — no sudden rises, no dramatic crashes.

All tracks were generated with AI music models, then processed for the final sound. No
