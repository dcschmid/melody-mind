## About This Album

The album opens with "Berlin brennt leise" — quiet burning, no headlines. Track 2, "Miete
frisst Träume", is where the record finds its thesis: rent eats dreams, always has.
"Pflegenotstand" takes on the healthcare collapse that Germany pretends isn't happening.
"Alte Republik, junge Wut" closes the record with the sharpest line on the album — old
republic, young rage.

The production stays in the same register throughout: cold synths, heavy 808s, late-night
piano that sounds like a train announcement nobody's listening to anymore. It doesn't try
to sound optimistic and it doesn't reach for party-rap energy. Twelve tracks, twelve
fragments of a country negotiating with itself.

The lyrics are street-level throughout. Crowded apartment viewings, delayed trains,
exhausted nurses, endless forms. The tracks don't lecture — they observe.
"Filterblasenland" is the sharpest cut; "Zwei Klassen, eine Stadt" is the one that stays
longest.

## Production Notes

All tracks were generated with AI music models, then processed for the final sound. No
human performance recordings are used.
