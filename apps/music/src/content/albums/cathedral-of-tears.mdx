## About the Album

Cathedral of Tears uses gothic scale, but the album works because it does not treat
sadness as scenery. The bells, choirs, iron doors, and marble figures give the record its
architecture. The emotional center is smaller: a voice standing inside all that stone and
trying not to break.

The first half is heavy with ritual. "The Bells Beneath the Rain" and "Before the Iron
Door" frame grief as something formal, almost liturgical. "Saint of the Broken Heart" and
"Marble Angels Weep" bring the drama closer to the body, where the religious imagery
starts to feel like a way of naming private pain.

The production should be heard for its depth rather than its darkness alone. Baritone
lines, female choirs, organ-like textures, and slow percussion give the songs room to
resonate. At its best, Cathedral of Tears feels less like a haunted building and more like
the sound a person makes when memory becomes a place.
