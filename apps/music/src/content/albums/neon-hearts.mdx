## About This Album

Thirteen tracks. Each one sounds like a different corner of Seoul at 4am: sometimes
edged, sometimes soft, always trapped between the speakers. The production leans on
sharp synths, deep bass, vocals placed over beats like headlights across an empty
highway. Some tracks push with danceability; others stretch out and breathe.

Thematically, the album stays tight to its concept: night, city, the feeling after.
No large statements. The lyrics work through suggestion — a name, a time, an
intersection. Vocals carry the melody; the instrumentation sets the mood.

「Our Last Light」 closes the album before dawn breaks. 「Lights of Seoul」 opens
with a synth riff that reads like a signal from a distance. The middle of the album
belongs to the slower pieces — "Beyond the Window" and "Mirror Night" breathe noticeably
less than the opening tracks.

The strength of Neon Hearts is its coherence. Thirteen tracks, none of them breaking
the tonality entirely. The weakness: for all its consistency, the album occasionally
sounds like a template — the structure from track to track repeats noticeably. That
works for a concept album; as an expression of individual voices, less so.

**Production:** All tracks were generated with AI music models and processed for the
final sound. No human performance recordings are used.
