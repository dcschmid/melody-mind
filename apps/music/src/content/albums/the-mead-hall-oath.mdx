## About This Album

The Mead Hall Oath runs fourteen tracks and follows the arc of an oath from celebration to
collapse. "Fire Over Frost" opens the album as a scene-setter: a hall lit by fire, horns
raised, loyalty declared. "The Mead Hall Oath" works as the thematic core. "Brothers
Beneath the Boar Banner" and "Ale, Iron, and Oaken Thrones" build the world further before
"Whispers Under the Beam" introduces the first tension. Track six, "The Smiling Traitor,"
is the pivot point where the narrative turns. "Blood on the Long Table" executes the
violence. "Widow at the Winter Gate" shifts to aftermath. "Ravens Over the Fjord" and "The
Wolf Does Not Forgive" track the hunt for the oathbreaker. "No Peace for Oathbreakers" is
the climax. "Ashes in the High Seat," "Feast for the Fallen," and "Saga Carved in Smoke"
close the album as epilogue.

The production uses AI-generated source material processed into melodic guitar-driven
Viking metal. Stomp rhythms, rough vocal takes, chant-ready choruses on tracks like "The
Wolf Does Not Forgive." "No Peace for Oathbreakers" is the longest track and the narrative
climax. "Blood on the Long Table" is the shortest. The two closing tracks run the same
length — the album doesn't end on a cliff but it doesn't resolve cleanly either.

If you have heard other MelodyMind metal releases, the workflow is the same: AI audio
generation shaped into final mixes. The Viking metal framing is consistent with the
project's approach to thematic albums.

## Production Notes

All tracks were generated with AI music models, then processed for the final sound. No
human performance recordings are used.
