## About the Album

Fleurs Fanées is a small-room record, and that is its strength. The album does not chase a
grand breakup narrative. It stays with objects: flowers on a table, a scarf holding
perfume, messages left undeleted, rain at the window. Those details give the songs their
quiet authority.

The sound belongs to dream pop, but it avoids floating away completely. Soft guitars,
muted keyboards, and close vocals make the music feel domestic, as if the listener is
sitting in the room after the argument has ended. "La Chambre Après Toi" and "Ton Parfum
Dans L'Écharpe" are effective because they trust ordinary traces to carry the emotional
weight.

The record's sadness is plain rather than theatrical. Love has faded, but not disappeared,
and the album is careful with that difference. Fleurs Fanées is for the moment when a life
looks normal from the outside while every familiar object has become too specific to
touch.
