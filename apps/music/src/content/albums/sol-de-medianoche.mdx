## About This Album

Sol de Medianoche is an Afro-Cuban Jazz Lounge / Neo Latin album built for late-night
listening. Warm Afro-Cuban percussion, jazz harmony, piano, intimate Spanish-language
vocals, muted brass, nylon guitar, and modern lounge textures shape a quiet nocturnal
world of tropical air, candlelit rooms, moonlit streets, rooftop reflections, and private
emotion.

The album opens with “Brisa de Cristal,” a smooth introduction that feels like stepping
onto a rooftop lounge after midnight. Soft piano, brushed percussion, and warm rhythmic
movement create a scene of glass, light, and night air, setting the tone for an album
built on close detail and slow motion.

With “Luna en la Habana,” the record moves into a romantic moonlit cityscape. The song
captures the charm of old Havana at night: balconies, narrow streets, soft shadows, and
memories carried through warm air. Its nostalgic Neo Latin mood gives the album one of its
most vivid moments.

“Oro en la Sombra” brings darker elegance. Like gold glowing inside velvet darkness, the
track blends sensual rhythm, rich jazz colors, and restrained tension. It adds a deeper
shadow to the album, showing its secretive side.

The mood opens on “Velas sobre el Mar,” a coastal and romantic piece shaped by images of
candles near the ocean, moonlight on water, and quiet distance. Its airy atmosphere and
soft Latin pulse make it one of the album’s most spacious songs.

“Jardín de Humo” deepens the sensual side of the record with the feeling of a hidden
terrace garden filled with perfume, smoke, dim lanterns, and quiet conversation. The song
moves slowly, adding smoke, shadow, and mystery to the album’s midnight setting.

In “Café a las Doce,” the album becomes urban and reflective. The song evokes the final
quiet hour in an elegant café: empty glasses, golden lights, soft voices, and the
stillness that remains after most of the city has gone silent. It is warm, human, and
understated.

“Azul de Medianoche” shifts into a cooler, deeper shade of night. With its sleek mood, the
track feels like a midnight drive through a sleeping city, where moonlight and neon
reflections blur across dark streets. It adds a modern edge to the album.

The sensual core of the record appears in “Ritual de Seda,” a song built around softness,
silk, candlelight, and slow movement. Its refined groove and elegant restraint make it one
of the album’s most intimate moments, focused on control, texture, and quiet tension.

“Noches de Canela” brings warmth and romantic glow into the sequence. With its amber mood,
soft sway, and gentle Latin-jazz elegance, the song feels like candlelight on dark wood,
spice in the air, and a quiet smile across a table late at night.

The album turns mysterious again with “Bajo la Palma Negra,” a darker tropical lounge
piece filled with shadow, warm air, and the silhouettes of palms against the night. Its
slow rhythm gives the record a secretive depth.

“Espejos del Caribe” offers a reflective pause, evoking mirrored light, tropical night
air, polished interiors, and ocean warmth. The song feels calm, elegant, and dreamlike,
adding a Caribbean glow to the album’s night setting.

With “Sombra y Jazmín,” the listener enters a private, fragrant space. Jasmine in the
night air, soft shadows on stone walls, and quiet courtyard intimacy define the song’s
romantic mood. It is tender, mysterious, and full of small details.

“Cristales en la Lluvia” brings one of the album’s most melancholic moments. Rain on
glass, reflections on dark streets, and soft lamplight shape a song of longing and
stillness. It deepens the album without breaking its low-lit mood.

The album closes with “Rosa del Aire,” a luminous final piece that feels like the last
breeze before dawn. Carrying perfume, memory, tenderness, and afterglow, the song gives
the album a graceful ending rather than a dramatic finale.

Together, these fourteen songs make Sol de Medianoche feel like one night told through
rooms, streets, coastlines, terraces, shadows, and reflections. The album moves from
rooftop quiet and moonlit nostalgia to smoky gardens, midnight cafés, tropical mystery,
rain-soaked melancholy, and dawn-like tenderness.

Sol de Medianoche is music for late hours and low light. It fits romantic evenings, lounge
settings, dinner ambience, night drives, creative focus, quiet reflection, and listeners
who want Latin jazz with a modern nocturnal touch. Smooth, warm, sensual, and restrained,
the album keeps its volume low and its details close.
