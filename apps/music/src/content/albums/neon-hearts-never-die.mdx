## About This Album

Neon Hearts Never Die is a cinematic Classic Hair Metal / Glam Rock / Arena Rock concept
album set in the glowing streets of a fictional 1980s metropolis called Electric City. It
is a story about youth, ambition, heartbreak and the dangerous beauty of chasing a dream
that might save you , or destroy you.

The album follows a young rock band as they arrive in Electric City with nothing but
guitars, attitude and the belief that their songs can carry them beyond ordinary life.
From the explosive opener “Electric City Nights”, the band steps into a world of neon
boulevards, rain-soaked asphalt, sold-out clubs and endless possibility. The title anthem
“Neon Hearts Never Die” turns that dream into a stadium-sized declaration: love may break,
fame may fade, but the music remains.

Across the album, every song captures a different scene from this glittering rock-and-roll
story. “Lipstick on the Mirror” brings playful glam mystery and hotel-room chaos, while
“Wild Boys Don’t Cry” reveals the loneliness behind the leather-jacket confidence.
“Midnight Radio Love” tells a romantic late-night story through radio signals, phone calls
and longing, before “Backstage Angels” throws the listener into the wild energy behind the
curtain.

The darker middle section shows the cost of success. “Chrome and Broken Promises” explores
betrayal by the music industry, false contracts and shiny lies. “One More Night in Fire”
becomes the emotional power ballad of the album , one final night with a lost love before
the tour moves on. “Leather, Lace & Lies” adds danger, temptation and toxic attraction,
while “Runaway Stage Lights” captures the restless escape of life on the road.

As the glitter begins to fall, the album becomes more bittersweet. “When the Glitter
Falls” reflects on the silence after the concert, when the crowd is gone and the dream
suddenly feels fragile. “City of Fallen Stars” looks at all the broken dreamers who almost
made it, while “Hearts on the Highway” brings back hope through motion, memory and the
open road.

The finale, “Raise Your Hands to the Sky,” is the triumphant comeback moment: the band
returns to the biggest stage in Electric City, surrounded by lights, smoke, roaring fans
and the realization that every heartbreak, betrayal and lonely night has become part of
the song. It is the emotional closing statement of the album , as long as the music lives,
nothing is truly lost.

Musically, Neon Hearts Never Die is built around huge 80s-inspired guitar riffs, melodic
lead guitar solos, stadium-sized drums, glossy synth textures, romantic power-ballad
moments and big English vocal hooks designed for sing-along choruses. The sound is
euphoric, rebellious, romantic and bittersweet , a love letter to the golden age of arena
rock, neon nightlife and larger-than-life rock dreams.
