## About This Album

Crimson Sakura is a 13-track concept album built around the cherry blossom as a symbol of
beauty at the edge of collapse. Each track takes the listener through a different scene:
burning fortresses, palace gardens, battlefields at dusk. The track list moves from opener
"緋桜の誓い" through "朱の城壁" and "散華の雨," closing with "その花は灰へ."

The music pairs melodic metal guitar work with orchestral arrangement. Strings and brass
carry most of the harmonic weight; the guitars handle the rhythmic drive and melodic
leads. Tracks range from around 192 to 248 seconds. The longest is track 11,
"誰がための血," at 248 seconds. The shortest is track 9, "無情の冠," at 192 seconds.

The vocals are Japanese-language. Dynamic range is wide: verses sit quiet and restrained,
choruses open into full orchestral swell with the vocal line doubled or harmonized. Some
tracks use layered choir in the final third.

Lyrically, the album moves between personal grief — track 5, "君なき春" (Spring Without
You) — and larger scale loss — track 9, "無情の冠" (Crown of Merciless Fate). No track
glorifies battle. War appears as collapse, not triumph. The closing sequence carries the
most emotional weight and is where the orchestral writing is densest.

## Production Notes

All tracks were generated with AI music models, then processed for the final sound. No
human performance recordings are used.
