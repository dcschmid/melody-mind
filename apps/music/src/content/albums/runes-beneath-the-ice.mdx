## About This Album

Runes Beneath the Ice is a cinematic Viking folk and Nordic dark folk concept album by
Melody Mind Music, created as an atmospheric journey into ice, memory, ritual, and ancient
northern mystery. Instead of focusing on war or battle, the album moves slowly and
spiritually through frozen landscapes, forgotten settlements, sacred mountains,
raven-guided paths, and the silent power of a rune stone buried beneath a frozen fjord.

The story follows a young seer who begins hearing voices in her dreams, whispers rising
from below the snow, calling her toward a truth hidden deep under the ice. Guided by
ravens and visions, she crosses black winter forests, frozen halls, ritual fire circles,
and sacred places where the boundary between the living and the ancestral world becomes
thin. Every track feels like another step in her pilgrimage: from the first ghostly call
in “Whispers Under Snow”, through the omen-filled journey of “The Raven Path”, the lonely
beauty of “Daughter of the Fjord”, and the deep ceremonial darkness of “The Frozen Hall”.

Musically, the album blends tagelharpa, bowed lyre, frame drums, bone percussion, deep
male choirs, female ritual vocals, dark string drones, distant horns, wind, cracking ice,
snow ambience, and wordless Nordic-inspired chants. The sound is slow, cold, organic, and
cinematic, closer to a spiritual ritual in a frozen wilderness than a traditional Viking
metal album. Songs like “Antlers in the Snow” and “Ashes in the Snow Circle” bring
stronger ceremonial rhythm, while “Voices of the First Winter” and “Hymn for the Buried
Ancestors” lean into ritual ambient, sacred choir textures, and ancient atmosphere.

At the center of the album stands the revelation beneath the fjord: “The Rune Below the
Fjord”, “The Stone Remembers”, and “When the Ice Opens” form the dramatic heart of the
story, where the buried rune awakens and reveals that the past was never truly gone, only
frozen, waiting to be heard again. The finale, “Return Beneath the Northern Lights”,
closes the journey with peace, memory, and quiet hope, as the seer returns beneath the
aurora carrying the voices of the ice within her.

Runes Beneath the Ice is ideal for listeners who love atmospheric Viking music, Nordic
folk, ritual ambient, dark cinematic soundscapes, frozen nature imagery, ancient
mythology, ravens, fjords, runes, northern lights, and slow spiritual storytelling. It is
a cold, mystical and deeply immersive album — a soundtrack for snowbound landscapes,
forgotten magic, and the silence of the ancient North.

All tracks were generated with AI music models, then processed for the final sound. No
