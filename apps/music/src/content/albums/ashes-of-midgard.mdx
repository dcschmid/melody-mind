## About This Album

Ashes of Midgard is a dark, cinematic Viking Metal album about the death of a world once
shaped by gods, warriors, and sacred oaths. A broken northern realm collapses beneath
fire, frost, and silence. The old halls burn, the skies darken, and even the memory of
glory fades into ash.

Crushing mid-tempo riffs, thunderous drums, mournful lead guitars, deep commanding vocals,
fierce growls, and vast choral textures balance raw metallic force with epic atmosphere.
The album is about war, but also about what comes after it: grief, memory, brotherhood,
pride, and the terrible beauty of standing tall at the end of all things. Each song feels
like a chapter in a final saga. Battle-scarred landscapes, ruined strongholds, black snow,
burning longships, and the silent remains of a dying kingdom move the story forward.

The heart of the album is its emotional contrast. Fierce but sorrowful, majestic but
broken, heroic but never triumphant in any simple way. It does not celebrate conquest. It
honors endurance. It does not glorify power. It gives weight to loss. The warriors do not
march toward easy victory. They walk into the end of their world with iron resolve,
carrying their oaths, their dead, and the last sparks of their identity into the storm.
That tension between collapse and dignity gives the album its soul.

The record draws on the grand scale and earthy power of classic Viking Metal, while
embracing a more cinematic sense of space. Massive choruses rise like funeral hymns over
scorched landscapes. Guitars move between crushing heaviness and cold, melancholic melody.
Drums hit like distant thunder over mountains and ruined coasts. Choirs and layered
textures deepen the mythic feeling, giving the album the weight of ancient legend rather
than modern aggression alone. The result is immersive, dramatic, and deeply visual. Every
track feels carved from firelight, smoke, iron, and winter wind.

Lyrically and conceptually, the album explores downfall, fate, shattered faith, ancestral
memory, and the final dignity of a people who refuse to disappear quietly. A world where
the gods no longer answer. Where banners still rise over broken stone. Where the last
survivors of a fallen age sing not for rescue, but for remembrance. Apocalypse lives here,
but so does reverence. Destruction, but also identity. The album becomes more than a tale
of mythic collapse. It becomes a meditation on what remains when greatness has burned
away.

Ashes of Midgard is for listeners who want Viking Metal with weight, atmosphere, and
narrative depth. An album that feels vast, tragic, and unforgettable. The sound of a
northern world burning beneath storm-black skies. Of voices still rising through the
ashes.
