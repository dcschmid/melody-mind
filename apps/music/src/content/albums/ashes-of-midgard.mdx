## About This Album

The album opens with the title track "Ashes of Midgard" and ends with "Beyond the End of
Midgard" — twelve chapters, one saga. The vocal contrast is the most distinctive choice:
clean singing carries the storytelling, growls sit underneath for the violence of the
world itself. Neither one dominates throughout.

Track 3, "The Wolves of the Last Winter", is the harshest and most primal cut. Track 7,
"The Fires of Bifröst", moves closest to the cosmic scale the album reaches for. "When
Ravens Circle" and "No Throne for the Dead" pull back into memory and aftermath. The back
half of the record builds toward the closer, which the album frames as silence beyond fire
rather than just another climax.

Musically, the record stays in the same register: heavy rhythm guitars, melodic leads that
cut through the bottom end, drums that feel ritualistic rather than technical. The
production is cold, weathered, layered — not clean, not sterile. Everything sounds like it
was recorded in a stone hall, not a studio.

The album is for listeners who want twelve tracks played in sequence. Not shuffled.

## Production Notes

All tracks were generated with AI music models, then processed for the final sound. No
human performance recordings are used.
