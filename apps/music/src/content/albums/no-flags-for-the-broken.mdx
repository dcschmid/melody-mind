## About This Album

No Flags for the Broken is an AI-assisted concept album by Melody Mind Music, shaped by
Street Punk, Political Punk, and Hardcore Punk. It is loud, angry, and direct: a record
made for raised fists, cracked streets, basement shows, protest marches, and voices that
refuse to stay silent.

The album takes place in a world where ordinary people are trapped between systems that
demand loyalty but give nothing back. Governments call for sacrifice, corporations profit
from crisis, nationalist movements sell fear as identity, and political leaders turn
poverty, war, and division into tools of control. Here, the broken are not weak. They are
workers, outsiders, families, young people, and survivors who realize that no flag, party,
crown, or ideology has truly stood for them.

Musically, the album is built on fast distorted guitars, raw bass, pounding drums, shouted
lead vocals, and big gang-vocal choruses. The sound is intentionally rough and human,
closer to a sweaty underground punk show than polished radio rock. The songs favor short,
sharp verses, chantable hooks, aggressive breakdowns, and an atmosphere of smoke, sirens,
concrete, anger, and solidarity.

The opening title track, “No Flags for the Broken,” sets the tone with a declaration that
the people at the bottom will no longer be used by governments, borders, corporations, or
political myths. “Borders Made of Blood” turns the focus toward nationalism and the human
cost of lines drawn on maps, while “Your War, Our Graves” attacks the machinery of war and
the way ordinary lives are spent for the ambitions of the powerful.

With “The Rich Sleep Well,” the album shifts into bitter class anger, pointing at a world
where luxury is protected while poverty is ignored. “Sirens Over Parliament” brings a more
chaotic, cinematic feeling, capturing the sound of a city erupting outside the walls of
political power. “Plastic Patriots” mocks fake patriotism and empty slogans, tearing into
symbolic loyalty that ignores real suffering.

At the heart of the album, “We Don’t March for Kings” is a refusal to obey rulers,
billionaires, generals, and false heroes. “Smoke Above the City” adds a darker emotional
layer, showing the aftermath of unrest and the exhaustion that follows rebellion. “Poor
Man’s Anthem” turns poverty and frustration into a communal street-punk hymn, full of
grit, heart, and working-class defiance.

The second half gets more confrontational. “Tear Down the Golden Walls” attacks elite
power and gated wealth with hardcore intensity, while “Children of the Riot” speaks for a
generation raised under crisis, propaganda, austerity, and broken promises. “Ashes in the
Underpass” looks at the forgotten people surviving beneath the slogans and speeches,
adding a bleak but compassionate street-level perspective.

Near the end, “Hands Together, Walls Apart” turns the album’s rage toward solidarity. It
is not about blind hope. It is about people finding each other beyond borders, class
divisions, and political manipulation. “Still Standing in the Ruins” closes the album as a
wounded protest hymn: people standing together after everything has burned, refusing to
disappear.

No Flags for the Broken is an angry punk album about disillusionment, resistance, and the
painful question of what remains when people no longer believe in the systems built above
them. Its answer is plain: each other.

Created, curated, and produced as an AI-assisted music project by Melody Mind Music.
