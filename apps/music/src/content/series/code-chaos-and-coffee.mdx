Code, Chaos & Coffee takes place at Infinite Loop Solutions, a software company sustained
by exhausted employees, undocumented systems, emergency coffee, and temporary fixes that
have long outlived their authors. The series turns ordinary IT failures into a connected
rock and metal comedy.

Each album moves to a different part of the company. System administrators guard forgotten
servers in the basement. SRE engineers answer production alerts after midnight. Helpdesk
workers face tickets that contradict themselves, while developers, testers, and Scrum
Masters negotiate releases, shifting priorities, and meetings arranged to explain other
meetings. Ransomware, cloud migrations, compliance audits, and unsafe production access
supply the larger disasters.

The jokes depend on recognizable technical details rather than a generic office setting. A
cold coffee, an inherited password, or an unexplained service can become the center of a
song. Recurring employees and unresolved systems link the albums, allowing one
department's shortcut to become another department's emergency.

Hard rock and heavy metal provide the series' base, with punk, industrial, gothic, and
progressive elements assigned to different teams and crises. Beneath the parody is a clear
sympathy for the people doing the maintenance. Management creates much of the chaos; the
workers who receive the urgent ticket are the ones left to restore order.
