Code, Chaos & Coffee is a rock and metal parody series set inside Infinite Loop Solutions,
a software company held together by exhausted employees, undocumented systems, emergency
coffee and an alarming number of temporary fixes.

Across five main albums and several companion stories, the series transforms everyday life
in IT into an exaggerated corporate rock opera. System administrators guard forgotten
servers beneath the office. SRE engineers answer production alerts in the middle of the
night. Helpdesk workers battle impossible support tickets, while developers, testers and
Scrum Masters attempt to survive shifting priorities, broken releases and meetings about
other meetings.

Every album focuses on a different corner of the company. Stories range from ransomware
attacks and disastrous cloud migrations to legacy systems that nobody dares to switch off.
There are romantic merge conflicts, compliance audits, aggressive software vendors and a
dangerously undertrained intern with administrator access to production.

The comedy is driven by familiar workplace frustrations, technical disasters and absurd
management decisions. Beneath the parody, however, the series also celebrates the people
who quietly keep modern companies running. Its administrators, support agents, developers,
QA engineers and operations teams are rarely treated as corporate heroes, yet they
repeatedly save Infinite Loop Solutions from problems created far above their pay grade.

Musically, the series moves between hard rock, heavy metal, progressive metal, punk rock,
industrial metal, gothic metal and melodic arena rock. Each album adapts its sound to its
department and central catastrophe while remaining part of the same interconnected
universe.

With recurring characters, escalating disasters and countless IT-related running jokes,
Code, Chaos & Coffee is a musical tribute to everyone who has ever received a ticket
marked “urgent,” joined a pointless meeting or heard the immortal sentence: “It worked
yesterday.”
