## How Did Jazz Become Neo Soul? The Laboratory Never Closed

Miles Davis. John Coltrane. D'Angelo. Erykah Badu.

**They all used the same musical laboratory, just in different rooms.**

In this 78-minute journey, Daniel and Annabelle trace how jazz evolved from postwar modal experiments through fusion and acid jazz into neo soul's intimate reclamation of vulnerability.

## What You'll Discover

**You'll learn:**

- How modal jazz opened structural freedom for modern music
- Why Fusion wasn't betrayal - it was evolution under pressure
- How Acid Jazz brought jazz back to the dance floor
- Why Neo Soul made vulnerability central to R&B

**You'll explore:**

- **Modal Revolution** - Miles Davis and John Coltrane redefining space, harmony, and trust
- **Electric Expansion** - Herbie Hancock, Wayne Shorter, Weather Report reshaping rhythm and identity
- **Acid Jazz Return** - London club culture and hip hop sampling reviving jazz grooves
- **Neo Soul Intimacy** - D'Angelo, Erykah Badu, Lauryn Hill, Maxwell transforming jazz language inward
- **Modern Continuity** - Robert Glasper, Esperanza Spalding, Thundercat, Hiatus Kaiyote carrying the lineage forward

## The Real Story

This isn't a genre overview.

**It's a listening journey through: Space → Electricity → Groove → Intimacy → Continuity.**

Jazz did not disappear. It adapted to amplification, studio technology, club culture, hip hop production, and digital streaming. Each transformation carried risk, shaped by pressure around race, visibility, control, and authorship.

And yet, the core habits survived:

**Listening. Collaboration. Space. Groove.**

The laboratory never closed. It just changed rooms.
