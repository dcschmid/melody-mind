## How Did Jazz Become Neo Soul? The Groove Kept Changing Shape

Miles Davis. John Coltrane. D'Angelo. Erykah Badu.

**They were not making the same music, but they were working inside the same continuum.**

In this 78-minute journey, Daniel and Annabelle trace how jazz moved from swing and bebop
into modal space, electric fusion, acid-jazz club culture, jazz rap, and finally the
intimate, groove-heavy language of neo soul.

## The Real Story

This is not just a genre overview.

**It is a listening journey through space, electricity, groove, intimacy, and
continuity.**

Jazz did not disappear. It adapted to amplification, studio production, club culture, hip
hop sampling, institutional memory, and digital circulation. Each transformation carried
pressure around race, economics, visibility, authorship, and who gets remembered.

And yet, the core habits survived:

**Listening. Collaboration. Space. Groove.**

The laboratory never closed. It changed rooms, tools, audiences, and expectations.
