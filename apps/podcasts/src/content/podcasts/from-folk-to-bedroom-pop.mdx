## How Did Folk Become Bedroom Pop?

Before playlists and home studios, songs moved through memory, labor, ritual, and
collective singing. Much later, they would move through vinyl, cassettes, laptops, and
phones.

**The voice kept getting closer.**

In this 77-minute episode, Daniel and Annabelle follow that long shift from shared folk
tradition to personal authorship, indie fragility, and the intimate digital worlds of
bedroom pop.

## The Real Story

The voice did not simply become quieter. **It became more individualized, more portable,
and more exposed.**

This is not just a story about style. It is a story about where songs live, who gets heard
inside changing systems, and how intimacy moved from community singing to direct address,
then into algorithmic circulation.

What stays constant is the human need for a voice that feels close.
