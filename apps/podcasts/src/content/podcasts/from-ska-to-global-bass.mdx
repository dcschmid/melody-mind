## How Did a Small Island Reshape Global Music?

Bob Marley. King Tubby. Major Lazer.

**They all started with the same thing: Jamaican rhythm.**

In this 95-minute journey, Daniel and Annabelle follow one of the most influential rhythmic pathways in modern music, from Kingston sound systems to global dance floors.

## What You'll Discover

**You'll learn:**

- How Jamaican musicians transformed American R&B into ska
- Why dub producers made the studio into an instrument
- How sound system culture spread across continents
- Why Jamaican bass philosophy still shapes electronic music today

**You'll explore:**

- **Ska's Birth** - Prince Buster, Coxsone Dodd, The Skatalites creating Jamaica's first distinctive sound
- **Rocksteady Groove** - Alton Ellis, The Paragons slowing the tempo, emphasizing bass
- **Reggae Goes Global** - Bob Marley, Toots and the Maytals, Jimmy Cliff bringing Jamaican voice to the world
- **Dub Revolution** - King Tubby, Lee "Scratch" Perry, Augustus Pablo transforming the studio into creative space
- **UK Sound Systems** - Linton Kwesi Johnson, Dennis Bovell, Aswad carrying bass culture to London
- **Dancehall Digital Era** - King Jammy, Yellowman, Shabba Ranks, Sister Nancy, Lady Saw
- **Global Bass Continuation** - Massive Attack, Major Lazer, Buraka Som Sistema proving Jamaican rhythm never stopped evolving
- **Modern Reggae** - Chronixx, Koffee reconnecting new generations with roots

## The Real Story

What started as music for neighborhood sound systems reshaped global music culture.

**From the energetic pulse of ska to the deeper groove of rocksteady, from reggae's spiritual voice to dub's studio experiments, from dancehall's digital revolution to global bass's borderless rhythms - Jamaican music kept evolving while carrying its history forward.**

The rhythm slowed. The bass deepened. The influence spread.

And it never stopped moving.
