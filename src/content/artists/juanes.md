biography: Juan Esteban Aristizábal Vázquez, known professionally as Juanes, born on August 9, 1972, in Medellín, Colombia, is a Colombian singer, songwriter, and guitarist who has become one of the most successful and influential Latin American artists of his generation, known for his blend of rock, pop, and Latin rhythms with socially conscious lyrics. He rose to prominence as the lead singer of the band Ekhymosis before launching his solo career in 2000 with the album Fíjate Bien, which won multiple Latin Grammy Awards and established him as a major force in Latin music. His subsequent albums, including Mi Sangre (2004), which became one of the best-selling Latin albums of all time, and his 2012 album Juanes, have showcased his evolution as an artist and his commitment to social causes including landmine awareness and education. Juanes has won numerous awards and has helped bring Latin rock to international audiences.
