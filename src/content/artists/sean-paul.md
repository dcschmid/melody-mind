biography: Sean Paul Henriques, born on January 9, 1973, in Kingston, Jamaica, is a Jamaican dancehall singer, songwriter, and producer who achieved global success in the early 2000s, known for his distinctive deep voice and dancehall-inflected pop hits that brought the genre to mainstream audiences worldwide. He rose to prominence with his 2002 album Dutty Rock, which featured the hit singles Get Busy and Like Glue and established him as a major force in bringing dancehall music to international audiences. His subsequent albums, including The Trinity (2005) and Imperial Blaze (2009), have continued to showcase his signature blend of dancehall rhythms with pop and hip-hop production. Sean Paul has sold over 60 million records worldwide and has influenced the global spread of dancehall music.
