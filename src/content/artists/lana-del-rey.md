biography: Elizabeth Woolridge Grant, born on June 21, 1985, in New York City, known professionally as Lana Del Rey, is an American singer and songwriter known for her cinematic, vintage-inspired sound, melancholic lyrics, and distinctive vocal style that has influenced a generation of artists and sparked debates about authenticity in pop music. She rose to prominence in 2011 with the single Video Games and her debut album Born to Die, which became a global phenomenon and established her as a defining artist of the decade with its blend of pop, baroque pop, and indie pop elements. Her subsequent albums including Ultraviolence (2014), Honeymoon (2015), and Norman Fucking Rockwell! (2019) have been critically acclaimed, with the latter being widely considered one of the greatest albums of the 2010s. Del Rey has influenced contemporary pop music with her aesthetic vision and willingness to defy conventions.
