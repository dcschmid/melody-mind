biography: Beyonce Giselle Knowles-Carter, born on September 4, 1981, in Houston, Texas, is an American singer, songwriter, producer, and actress widely regarded as one of the greatest entertainers of all time, known for her powerful vocals, innovative performances, and influence on popular culture spanning over two decades. She rose to fame as the lead singer of Destiny's Child, one of the best-selling female groups of all time, before launching her solo career in 2003 with the album Dangerously in Love, which established her as a dominant force in pop and R&B music. Her subsequent albums B'Day (2006), I Am... Sasha Fierce (2008), and Beyonce (2013) have all achieved critical and commercial success, with her 2022 album Renaissance breaking streaming records. Beyonce has won 32 Grammy Awards, the most by any artist, and her influence on music, fashion, and social activism has made her a global cultural icon.
