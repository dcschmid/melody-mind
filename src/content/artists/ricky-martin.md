biography: Ricky Martin, born Enrique Martín Morales, on December 24, 1971, in San Juan, Puerto Rico, is a Puerto Rican singer, songwriter, and actor who became a global Latin pop superstar in the late 1990s and early 2000s, known for his energetic performances, pop anthems, and status as one of the most successful Latin artists of all time. He began his career as a child in the boy band Menudo before launching a solo career that achieved massive international success with albums like Vuelve (1998) and Ricky Martin (1999), which featured the global hit Livin' la Vida Loca. Martin has sold over 70 million records worldwide and has successfully maintained his career across multiple decades while expanding into acting and philanthropy. His influence on Latin pop and his status as a cultural ambassador for Puerto Rico remain significant.
