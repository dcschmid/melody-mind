biography: Demetria Devonne Lovato, born on August 20, 1992, in Dallas, Texas, is an American singer, songwriter, and actress known for their powerful vocals, willingness to address mental health and advocacy issues, and evolution from Disney star to respected recording artist. They began their career as a child actor on Barney & Friends before launching a music career with the album Don't Forget (2008), which established them as a pop-rock artist. Subsequent albums including Here We Go Again (2009), Confident (2015), and Tell Me You Love Me (2017) showcased their artistic evolution and vocal prowess. Lovato has been open about their struggles with mental health, addiction, and identity, becoming an advocate for mental health awareness and LGBTQ+ rights while maintaining a successful music career.
