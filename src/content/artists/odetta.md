biography: Odetta Holmes, known mononymously as Odetta, was a folk singer, actress, and civil rights activist whose powerful voice and repertoire of traditional folk songs made her a central figure in the folk music revival of the 1950s and 1960s. Born in Birmingham, Alabama, she studied classical music and dance before discovering folk music in New York City. Her deep, resonant voice and her commitment to traditional songs and social justice made her an icon of the folk movement. She performed at the 1963 March on Washington and influenced generations of folk and rock musicians.
