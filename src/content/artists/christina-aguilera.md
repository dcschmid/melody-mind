biography: Christina María Aguilera, born on December 18, 1980, in Staten Island, New York, is an American singer, songwriter, actress, and television personality known for her powerful four-octave vocal range, vocal virtuosity, and versatility across pop, R&B, and classical music genres. She rose to prominence in 1999 with her self-titled debut album and the hit single Genie in a Bottle, establishing her as a major force in pop music and earning her multiple Grammy Awards. Her 2002 album Stripped showcased her artistic growth with more personal and diverse musical influences, while her 2010 album Bionic demonstrated her embrace of electronic and dance music. Aguilera has sold over 75 million records worldwide and has been praised for her technical vocal ability and willingness to experiment with different musical styles.
