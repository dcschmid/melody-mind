biography: Patsy Cline was one of the most influential and acclaimed vocalists in country music history, known for her emotive, contralto voice and her ability to cross genre boundaries. Born Virginia Patterson Hensley in Virginia, she began performing in her teens and quickly gained recognition for her powerful, nuanced singing style. Her brief career, cut short by a tragic plane crash, produced a handful of recordings that have become enduring classics. Clines influence extended far beyond country music, affecting pop, rock, and crossover artists for generations.
