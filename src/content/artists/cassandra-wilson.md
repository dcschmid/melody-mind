biography: Cassandra Wilson, born on November 4, 1955, in Jackson, Mississippi, is an American jazz singer, songwriter, and producer known for her distinctive contralto voice and innovative approach to jazz that incorporates elements of blues, country, folk, and pop music. She began her career in the 1980s as a jazz bassist and vocalist, releasing several acclaimed albums before achieving mainstream success with her 1995 album New Moon Daughter, which featured her distinctive interpretations of standards and original compositions. Her 1996 follow-up album Traveling Miles, a tribute to Miles Davis, further established her as one of the most important and influential jazz vocalists of her generation. Wilson has won multiple Grammy Awards and has been praised for her ability to bring jazz to wider audiences while maintaining artistic integrity.
