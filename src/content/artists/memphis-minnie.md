biography: Memphis Minnie was one of the most influential female blues guitarists and vocalists of the pre-war era. Born Lizzie Douglas in Algiers, Louisiana, she ran away to Memphis as a teenager and became a fixture on Beale Street. Known for her distinctive fingerpicking guitar style and powerful voice, she recorded over 80 songs between 1929 and 1935. Her innovative guitar work influenced generations of musicians, and she was one of the few female blues artists to achieve commercial success during the 1920s and 1930s. Despite facing racial and gender discrimination, she maintained a prolific recording career and remained active until her death.
