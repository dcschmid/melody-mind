biography: Björk Guðmundsdóttir, born on November 21, 1965, in Reykjavík, Iceland, is an Icelandic singer, songwriter, producer, and actress renowned for her eclectic musical style that spans experimental electronic music, pop, jazz, and classical arrangements, as well as her innovative approach to music production and performance art. She began her career at age 11 with the album Björk (1977) and gained international fame as the lead vocalist of The Sugarcubes before launching her solo career with Debut (1993), which established her signature blend of electronic beats, jazz influences, and avant-garde production techniques. Her 1997 album Homogenic and 2001 album Vespertines are widely considered masterpieces of electronic music, while her willingness to push artistic boundaries has made her one of the most influential and respected artists of her generation.
