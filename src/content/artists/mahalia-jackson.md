biography: Mahalia Jackson was one of the most influential gospel singers of the 20th century, known for her powerful, soul-stirring voice and her role in bringing gospel music to mainstream audiences. Born in New Orleans, she began singing in church as a child and developed her distinctive, emotionally charged style through gospel music. Her deep, resonant voice and heartfelt performances earned her the title "Queen of Gospel." Jackson's influence extended far beyond gospel, affecting R&B, soul, and popular music artists worldwide.
