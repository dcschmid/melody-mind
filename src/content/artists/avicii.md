biography: Tim Bergling, known professionally as Avicii, born on September 8, 1989, in Stockholm, Sweden, and died on April 20, 2018, was a Swedish DJ, record producer, and songwriter who rose to prominence in the early 2010s as one of EDM's most successful and influential artists, known for his blend of electronic dance music with pop and progressive house elements. He achieved breakthrough success with the single Levels (2012), which became a global dance anthem and established his signature sound. His subsequent hits including Wake Me Up!, Hey Brother, and The Nights brought electronic music to mainstream audiences worldwide. Avicii released two studio albums, True (2013) and Stories (2015), which showcased his evolution as an artist beyond pure EDM. His struggles with mental health and subsequent death at age 28 brought attention to the pressures faced by artists in the electronic music industry.
