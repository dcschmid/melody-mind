biography: Ellas Otha Bates, born on December 30, 1928, in McComb, Mississippi, known professionally as Bo Diddley, was an American musician and songwriter who became one of the most innovative and influential figures in early rock and roll, known for his distinctive rectangular guitar, rhythmic innovations, and raw, driving sound that helped define the beat at the heart of rock music. He rose to prominence in 1955 with his self-titled debut single and the hit Bo Diddley, which introduced his famous rhythm pattern that became known as the Bo Diddley beat, a clave-based rhythm that influenced countless songs across multiple genres. His raw, driving performances and innovative use of rhythm helped establish the foundation for rock and roll, while his eccentricity and showmanship influenced generations of performers. Diddley's impact on rock music is immeasurable, with his rhythmic innovations and attitude shaping the sound of rock, punk, and alternative music.
