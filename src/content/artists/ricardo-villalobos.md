biography: Ricardo Villalobos, born on January 17, 1970, in Santiago, Chile, is a Chilean-born German DJ and producer widely regarded as one of the most innovative and influential figures in minimal techno and microhouse music, known for his lengthy, exploratory DJ sets and minimalist approach to production. He began his DJ career in the early 1990s and rose to prominence in the 2000s with releases on labels like Perlon and Cocoon, helping define the sound of minimal techno. His 2004 album Alcachofa and 2006 album Dexter were critically acclaimed for their innovative use of space, rhythm, and sampling. Villalobos has become a fixture at clubs and festivals worldwide, with his marathon DJ sets and distinctive approach to electronic music influencing generations of producers and DJs.
