biography: Shakira Isabel Mebarak Ripoll, born on February 2, 1977, in Barranquilla, Colombia, is a Colombian singer, songwriter, producer, and philanthropist widely regarded as one of the most successful and influential Latin artists of all time, known for her distinctive voice, hip-shaking dance moves, and blend of Latin, Arabic, and rock influences. She began her career as a child in Colombia before achieving breakthrough success in Latin America with albums like Pies Descalzos (1995) and ¿Dónde Estás Corazón? (1996). Her 2001 album Laundry Service, featuring the hit Whenever, Wherever, established her as a global superstar, and she has since sold over 70 million records worldwide. Shakira's influence extends beyond music into philanthropy, particularly through her Pies Descalzos schools in Colombia, and her status as a cultural ambassador for Latin music remains significant.
