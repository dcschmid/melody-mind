biography: Édith Piaf was France's most famous and enduring chanson singer, known for her powerful, emotionally intense performances and her distinctive waif-like appearance. Born Édith Giovanna Gassion in Paris, she began singing on the streets of Montmartre as a child and developed her dramatic, heartfelt style through years of live performance. Her interpretations of French standards and her original compositions have become definitive versions of the chanson genre. Despite a life marked by tragedy, injury, and addiction, Piaf created some of the most beloved music of the 20th century.
