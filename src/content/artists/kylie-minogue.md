biography: Kylie Minogue, born on May 28, 1968, in Melbourne, Australia, is an Australian singer, songwriter, and actress known as the Princess of Pop, who has maintained a successful music career spanning over four decades with her blend of dance-pop, electropop, and disco-influenced music. She rose to fame in the late 1980s through her role on the Australian soap opera Neighbours before launching a music career that produced hits like The Locomotion (1988), I Should Be So Lucky (1988), and Can't Get You Out of My Head (2001). Minogue has sold over 80 million records worldwide and has successfully reinvented her musical style multiple times throughout her career, maintaining relevance in an ever-changing music industry. Her influence on pop music and fashion, her status as a gay icon, and her resilience as an artist have made her one of the most enduring figures in popular music.
