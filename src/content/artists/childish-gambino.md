biography: Donald Glover, known professionally as Childish Gambino, born on September 25, 1983, in Edwards Air Force Base, California, is an American actor, writer, director, producer, and musician who has become one of the most innovative and influential multi-hyphenate artists of his generation, known for his genre-blending approach to hip-hop and R&B and his groundbreaking work in television and film. He began his music career as Childish Gambino in 2008 and achieved breakthrough success with the mixtape Royalty (2012) and albums including Because the Internet (2013) and Awaken, My Love! (2016), the latter of which marked a dramatic shift to funk and soul-influenced music. His 2018 single This Is America became a cultural phenomenon, winning multiple Grammy Awards and sparking discussions about race and violence in America. Glover has won numerous awards across multiple disciplines and has established himself as a visionary artist.
