biography: Dwayne Michael Carter Jr., known professionally as Lil Wayne, born on September 27, 1982, in New Orleans, Louisiana, is an American rapper, songwriter, and executive widely regarded as one of the most influential and successful rappers of his generation, known for his prolific output, distinctive voice, and influence on hip-hop culture spanning over two decades. He rose to prominence as a teenager in the late 1990s as a member of the Hot Boys before launching his solo career and eventually becoming the leader of Young Money Entertainment, one of hip-hop's most successful record labels. His 2008 album Tha Carter III sold over 1 million copies in its first week and won the Grammy Award for Best Rap Album, establishing him as one of hip-hop's biggest stars. Wayne has released numerous mixtapes and albums, influencing countless artists with his unique flow, wordplay, and ability to adapt to changing trends in hip-hop.
