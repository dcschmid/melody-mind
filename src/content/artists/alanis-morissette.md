biography: Alanis Nadine Morissette, born on June 1, 1974, in Ottawa, Ontario, Canada, is a Canadian-American singer-songwriter and musician who rose to fame in the mid-1990s with her breakthrough album Jagged Little Pill (1995), which became one of the best-selling albums of all time with over 33 million copies sold worldwide. The album featured a string of hits including You Oughta Know, Ironic, and Hand in My Pocket that established her as the defining voice of Generation X with their raw emotional honesty and angst-driven lyrics. Morissette's songwriting, which blended confessional lyrics with alternative rock and post-grunge production, earned her multiple Grammy Awards including Album of the Year and Best Female Rock Vocal Performance. Throughout her career, she has sold over 60 million records worldwide, demonstrating her ability to evolve musically while maintaining her distinctive voice and emotional authenticity.
