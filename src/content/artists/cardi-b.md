biography: Belcalis Marlenis Almánzar, known professionally as Cardi B, born on October 11, 1992, in The Bronx, New York City, is an American rapper, songwriter, and media personality who rose to prominence through social media before becoming one of the most successful female rappers of all time, known for her distinctive voice, unapologetic personality, and chart-topping hits. She achieved breakthrough success with the single Bodak Yellow (2017), which reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 and made her the first female rapper to achieve an unaccompanied number one hit since 1998. Her 2018 debut album Invasion of Privacy debuted at number one and won the Grammy Award for Best Rap Album, making her the first female artist to win in that category. Cardi B has sold over 30 million records worldwide and has become a cultural icon known for her authenticity and willingness to address social issues.
