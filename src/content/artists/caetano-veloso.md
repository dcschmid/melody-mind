biography: Caetano Emanuel Vianna Telles Veloso, born on August 7, 1942, in Santo Amaro da Purificacão, Bahia, Brazil, is a Brazilian singer, songwriter, and musician widely regarded as one of the most influential and important artists in Brazilian music history, known for his distinctive voice, sophisticated songwriting, and intellectual approach to tropicalia. He rose to prominence in the 1960s as a pioneer of the Tropicalia movement alongside Gilberto Gil, blending Brazilian popular music with rock, avant-garde, and international influences despite political persecution during Brazil's military dictatorship. His 1968 album Caetano Veloso was a landmark in Brazilian music, and he has continued to release critically acclaimed albums throughout his six-decade career, influencing countless artists across multiple genres and generations. Veloso has won multiple Grammy Awards and is recognized as a cultural ambassador for Brazil worldwide.
