He rose to prominence in the 1960s as a pioneer of the Tropicalia movement alongside Gilberto Gil, blending Brazilian popular music with rock, avant-garde, and international influences despite political persecution during Brazil's military dictatorship. His 1968 album Caetano Veloso was a landmark in Brazilian music, and he has continued to release critically acclaimed albums throughout his six-decade career, influencing countless artists across multiple genres and generations. Veloso has won multiple Grammy Awards and is recognized as a cultural ambassador for Brazil worldwide.
