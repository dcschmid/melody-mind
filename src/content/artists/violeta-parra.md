biography: Violeta Parra was a Chilean singer, songwriter, guitarist, and folklorist who became one of the most influential figures in Latin American folk music. Born in San Carlos, Chile, she began performing with her siblings in the 1950s and quickly developed her own distinctive style that blended traditional Chilean folk music with her own compositions. Her powerful voice, virtuoso guitar playing, and passionate advocacy for folk traditions made her a cultural icon throughout Latin America. Beyond her musical achievements, Parra was a poet, painter, and ethnographer who dedicated her life to preserving and promoting Chilean and Latin American folk culture.
