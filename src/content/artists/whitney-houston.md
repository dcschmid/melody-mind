biography: Whitney Elizabeth Houston, born on August 9, 1963, in Newark, New Jersey, was an American singer, actress, and producer widely regarded as one of the greatest vocalists in music history, known for her powerful mezzo-soprano voice, perfect pitch, and seamless incorporation of gospel influences into popular music. She began her career as a background vocalist for artists like Teddy Pendergrass and Luther Vandross before launching her solo career in 1985 with a self-titled album that established her as a major force in pop and R&B music. Her 1992 film The Bodyguard and its accompanying soundtrack, featuring her iconic cover of I Will Always Love You, became a global phenomenon, with the soundtrack becoming one of the best-selling of all time.
