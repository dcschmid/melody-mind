biography: Adele Laurie Blue Adkins, born on May 5, 1988, in Tottenham, London, England, is a British singer and songwriter widely regarded as one of the greatest vocalists of her generation, known for her powerful contralto voice, emotive songwriting, and ability to connect with audiences through deeply personal lyrics. She rose to prominence in 2008 with her debut album 19, which established her as a rising soul talent, but achieved global superstardom with her 2011 album 21, which became one of the best-selling albums of all time with over 45 million copies sold worldwide. The album's singles including Rolling in the Deep, Someone Like You, and Set Fire to the Rain dominated charts worldwide and showcased her ability to blend soul, pop, and jazz influences. Her 2015 album 25 continued her commercial success, with the single Hello becoming a global phenomenon. Adele has won 15 Grammy Awards and has been recognized for her contribution to music.
