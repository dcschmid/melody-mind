biography: Usher Raymond IV, born on October 14, 1978, in Dallas, Texas, is an American singer, songwriter, and actor known as the King of R&B, who has been one of the most successful and influential R&B artists of his generation, selling over 75 million records worldwide. He began his career as a teenager in the 1990s with his self-titled debut album and achieved breakthrough success with 8701 (2001), which featured the hits U Remind Me and U Don't Have to Call. His 2004 album Confessions sold over 10 million copies in the United States alone and is widely considered one of the greatest R&B albums of all time, featuring hits like Yeah!, My Boo, and Caught Up. Usher has won multiple Grammy Awards and has successfully maintained his status as an R&B icon while expanding into acting and business ventures.
