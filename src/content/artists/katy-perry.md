biography: Katheryn Elizabeth Hudson, born on October 25, 1984, in Santa Barbara, California, known professionally as Katy Perry, is an American singer and songwriter who rose to global superstardom in the late 2000s and early 2010s, becoming one of the best-selling music artists of all time with over 115 million records sold worldwide. She achieved breakthrough success with her 2008 album One of the Boys, which featured the hits I Kissed a Girl and Hot n Cold, establishing her as a pop icon known for her colorful persona and catchy pop songs. Her subsequent albums Teenage Dream (2010) and Prism (2013) achieved massive commercial success, with Teenage Dream becoming the first album by a female artist to produce five number one singles on the Billboard Hot 100. Perry has been praised for her ability to create memorable pop music and her willingness to embrace bold, theatrical presentations.
