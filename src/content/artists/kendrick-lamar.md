biography: Kendrick Lamar Duckworth, born on June 17, 1987, in Compton, California, is an American rapper, songwriter, and producer widely regarded as one of the greatest and most important rappers of his generation, known for his sophisticated storytelling, social commentary, and artistic vision that has redefined hip-hop. He rose to prominence with his 2011 album Section.80 and achieved mainstream breakthrough with good kid, m.A.A.d city (2012), which was critically acclaimed for its narrative approach to storytelling. His 2015 album To Pimp a Butterfly was widely considered a masterpiece, blending jazz, funk, and hip-hop while addressing issues of race, identity, and social justice. His 2017 album Damn won the Pulitzer Prize for Music, making him the first non-jazz or classical artist to receive the honor. Lamar has won 14 Grammy Awards and has influenced hip-hop with his artistic ambition.
