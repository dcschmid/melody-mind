biography: Alicia Augello Cook, known professionally as Alicia Keys, born on January 25, 1981, in Manhattan, New York City, is an American singer, songwriter, and pianist known for her soulful voice, piano-playing virtuosity, and blend of classical training with contemporary R-pop and hip-hop production. She rose to prominence in 2001 with her debut album Songs in A Minor, which sold over 12 million copies worldwide and earned her five Grammy Awards including Best New Artist and Song of the Year for Fallin'. Her innovative fusion of classical piano melodies with modern R&B, soul, and hip-hop elements established a new sound in popular music and influenced a generation of artists. Keys has sold over 65 million records worldwide and has expanded her career to include acting, philanthropy, and activism for various social causes.
