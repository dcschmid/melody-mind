biography: "Kwon Bo-ah, known professionally as BoA, born on November 13, 1986, in Seoul, South Korea, is a South Korean singer, songwriter, and actress who pioneered the Korean Wave (Hallyu) and became one of the most successful and influential Asian artists in music history. She debuted at age 13 with the album ID; Peace B (2000) and achieved breakthrough success across Asia with her Korean and Japanese language releases, becoming one of the few Korean artists to achieve significant commercial success in Japan. Her 2002 Japanese album Listen to My Heart was the first by a Korean artist to reach #1 on the Oricon charts, and she has sold over 25 million records worldwide. BoA's influence on K-pop's global expansion and her role as a mentor to subsequent generations of K-pop artists have made her an icon of the Korean music industry."
