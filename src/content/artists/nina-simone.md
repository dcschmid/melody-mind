biography: Nina Simone was an American singer, songwriter, musician, and civil rights activist whose work spanned jazz, blues, folk, R&B, gospel, and classical music. Born Eunice Kathleen Waymon in North Carolina, she began performing professionally in the 1950s after studying classical piano at Juilliard. Her rich, deep voice and powerful, emotionally charged performances earned her the title "High Priestess of Soul." Beyond her musical achievements, Simone became increasingly involved in the civil rights movement, using her platform to address social and political issues.
