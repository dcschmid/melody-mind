biography: Innocent Ujah Idibia, known professionally as 2Baba, formerly 2Face Idibia, born on September 18, 1975, in Jos, Nigeria, is a Nigerian singer, songwriter, and record producer widely regarded as one of Africa's most successful and influential musicians, known as the King of African Pop. He gained fame as a member of the group Plantashun Boiz in the late 1990s before launching a solo career that established him as a pan-African superstar with his 2004 album Face 2 Face. His blend of R&B, hip-hop, and traditional African rhythms has earned him numerous awards and made him a cultural ambassador for African music worldwide. Idibia has sold over 30 million records across Africa and has influenced a generation of African artists who have followed in his footsteps.

His upbringing in this culturally rich environment provided the foundation for his future blending of traditional African rhythms with contemporary R&B and hip-hop sounds."

Their formation came at a pivotal time when Nigerian music was experiencing a renaissance, creating opportunities for new artists to reach audiences hungry for homegrown talent."

This release marked the beginning of the Nigerian urban music movement and established Plantashun Boiz as pioneers of the emerging Afro-pop genre."

This transition marked a pivotal moment in his career, as he prepared to establish himself as a solo artist capable of carrying the Nigerian music industry forward."
