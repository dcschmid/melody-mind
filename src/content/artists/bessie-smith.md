biography: Bessie Smith was the most successful and highest-paid African American entertainer of the 1920s and 1930s, earning her the title "Empress of the Blues." Born in Chattanooga, Tennessee, she began her career performing on the vaudeville circuit and quickly rose to fame with her powerful contralto voice and emotionally charged performances. Her recording career spanned from 1923 to 1933, during which she made over 160 recordings. Smith played a crucial role in the development of blues music and influenced generations of singers across all genres. Her tragic death after a car accident in Clarksdale, Mississippi, marked the end of the classic blues era.
