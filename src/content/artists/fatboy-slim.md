biography: Norman William Cook, known professionally as Fatboy Slim, born on July 31, 1963, in Bromley, England, is an English DJ, record producer, and musician widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in electronic dance music, known for his infectious big beat sound, memorable music videos, and chart-topping hits that brought dance music to mainstream audiences. He achieved massive commercial success in the late 1990s and early 2000s with albums like Better Living Through Chemistry (1999) and Halfway Between the Gutter and the Stars (2000), which featured hits like Praise You and The Rockafeller Skank that became defining tracks of the era. His legendary parties, including the 2002 Big Beach Boutique II on Brighton Beach which attracted over 250,000 people, and his innovative music videos have made him a cultural icon whose influence on dance music and popular culture remains significant.
