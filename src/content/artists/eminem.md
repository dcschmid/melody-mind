biography:
  Marshall Bruce Mathers III, known professionally as Eminem, Slim Shady, or Marshall Mathers, born on October 17, 1972, in St. Joseph, Missouri, is an American rapper, songwriter, and record producer widely regarded as one of the greatest and most influential rappers of all time, known for his technical proficiency, complex rhyme schemes, and controversial lyrics that have sparked debate while also earning critical acclaim. He rose to prominence in the late 1990s with his major label debut The Slim Shady LP (1999), which established his alter ego Slim Shady and his distinctive blend of shock value, dark humor, and social commentary. His 2000 album The Marshall Mathers LP became the fastest-selling album in history at the time, and his subsequent albums have continued to achieve commercial and critical success. Eminem has sold over 220 million records worldwide, making him one of the best-selling music artists in history, and has successfully transitioned into film, entrepreneurship,
  and activism.
