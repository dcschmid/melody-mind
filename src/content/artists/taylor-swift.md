biography: Taylor Alison Swift, born on December 13, 1989, in Reading, Pennsylvania, is an American singer-songwriter known for her narrative songwriting, vocal abilities, and ability to reinvent herself across multiple genres, making her one of the most successful and influential artists of her generation with over 200 million records sold worldwide. She began her career in country music with her self-titled debut album (2006) and achieved breakthrough success with Fearless (2008), which became the best-selling album of 2009. Her subsequent albums including Red (2012), 1989 (2014), Reputation (2017), and Folklore (2020) have demonstrated her artistic evolution and commercial dominance across country, pop, and indie folk genres. Swift has won 12 Grammy Awards and has been recognized for her songwriting and ability to connect with audiences through deeply personal lyrics.
