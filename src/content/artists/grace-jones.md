biography: Grace Jones, born on May 19, 1948, in Spanish Town, Jamaica, is a Jamaican-American singer, actress, and model known for her androgynous appearance, powerful contralto voice, and avant-garde approach to music and performance. She began her career as a model in New York before transitioning to music in the 1970s, releasing disco albums that established her reputation for boundary-pushing performances and distinctive vocal style. Her 1981 album Nightclubbing and 1982s Slave to the Rhythm marked her artistic peak, featuring collaborations with producers Sly and Robbie, Jackie Mittoo, and guitarist Nile Rodgers.
