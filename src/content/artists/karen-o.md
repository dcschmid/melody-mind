biography: Karen Anne Orzolek, born November 22, 1978, in South Korea, adopted by American family and raised in Englewood, New Jersey, is an American singer, songwriter, and musician best known as the lead vocalist and creative force behind the Yeah Yeah Yeahs, one of the most influential indie rock bands of the 2000s. She formed Yeah Yeah Yeahs in 2000 and led the band to critical and commercial success with their debut album Fever to Tell (2003), which established her as a compelling and unconventional frontwoman with her powerful vocals, theatrical performances, and distinctive fashion sense. Her subsequent work with Yeah Yeah Yeahs, including the albums Show Your Bones (2006) and It's Blitz! (2009), demonstrated her artistic growth and versatility. Orzolek has also pursued successful solo projects and collaborations in film, theater, and fashion, establishing herself as a multifaceted artist whose influence extends beyond music into broader cultural contexts.
